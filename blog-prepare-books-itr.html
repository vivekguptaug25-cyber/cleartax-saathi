How to prepare your books before ITR filing season starts

A practical article on getting records, statements, deductions, and business books ready before return filing season becomes urgent.

Why preparation matters before filing season

Income tax return filing becomes stressful when books are updated only at the last minute. A smoother process begins weeks earlier by organizing income records, expense proofs, and bank reconciliations so your CA is not forced to build the story from scattered documents.

What to organize in advance

  • Updated sales and expense records for the full financial year.
  • Bank statements, loan statements, and interest certificates.
  • TDS certificates, Form 26AS checks, and AIS review.
  • Clear separation between personal spending and business spending.

How this saves time and tax disputes

Good preparation improves accuracy, helps identify allowable deductions, and makes it easier to respond if the department raises any query later. It also helps the advisor see the real financial picture instead of working through incomplete assumptions.

If your business is growing quickly, this preparation stage is often where stronger bookkeeping discipline begins.
