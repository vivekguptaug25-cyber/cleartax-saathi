GST Guide

GST registration checklist for new businesses in India

A founder-friendly overview of the essentials to prepare before you begin GST registration, from threshold clarity to documentation and approval mistakes.

When GST registration becomes necessary

GST registration requirements depend on the nature of your business, your turnover, and whether you are supplying within your state or across India. Many founders wait too long, while others register too early without understanding the compliance burden that follows.

Before starting the registration process, confirm whether your category of supply triggers mandatory registration, whether you are selling through e-commerce platforms, and whether your invoices, bank details, and business identity documents are aligned.

Documents you should keep ready

  • PAN card and Aadhaar details of the proprietor, partners, or directors.
  • Business address proof such as rent agreement, electricity bill, or ownership proof.
  • Bank statement or cancelled cheque with the legal business name.
  • Photograph, constitution documents, and authorization proof where applicable.

Common mistakes that delay approval

The most common delays happen when the legal name on the PAN does not match the bank account, the business address proof is incomplete, or the activity description is too vague. It is also important to choose the right registration type and HSN or SAC orientation from the beginning.

A guided registration process reduces rejection, speeds up approval, and sets up later filing in a cleaner way.
